Vincent van Gogh

Vincent van Gogh (1853-1890) was a Dutch Post-Impressionist painter who is among the most famous and influential figures in the history of Western art. In just over a decade, he created about 2,100 artworks, including around 860 oil paintings, most of which date from the last two years of his life. Despite his lack of success during his lifetime, he gained widespread critical, commercial, and popular success over the ensuing decades.

"Starry Night" is one of Vincent van Gogh's most famous paintings, created in 1889. It features a night sky filled with stars over a small town and a large cypress tree. The painting is known for its bold colors and emotional brushwork.

"Wheatfield with Crows" is one of Van Gogh's most famous paintings, created in 1890. It features a dark sky filled with crows above a wheatfield. The painting is seen as a reflection of Van Gogh's troubled state of mind.
