Leonardo da Vinci

Leonardo da Vinci (1452-1519) was an Italian polymath of the High Renaissance who is widely considered one of the most diversely talented individuals ever to have lived. His areas of interest included invention, drawing, painting, sculpture, architecture, science, music, mathematics, engineering, literature, anatomy, geology, astronomy, botany, paleontology, and cartography. Leonardo is widely regarded as one of the most diversely talented individuals ever to have lived.

"Mona Lisa" is one of the most famous paintings in the world. Created by Leonardo da Vinci in the early 16th century, it features a portrait of a woman with a mysterious smile. The painting is renowned for its exquisite detail and the enigmatic expression of the subject.

"The Last Supper" is a mural painting by Leonardo da Vinci, created between 1495 and 1498. It depicts the moment Jesus announces that one of his disciples will betray him. The painting is famous for its composition and the emotional reactions of the disciples.
