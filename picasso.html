Pablo Picasso

Pablo Picasso (1881–1973) was a Spanish painter, sculptor, printmaker, ceramicist, and stage designer who spent most of his adult life in France. He is widely regarded as one of the most influential artists of the 20th century, co-founding the Cubist movement and inventing constructed sculpture. Picasso's prolific output includes over 20,000 paintings, drawings, sculptures, ceramics, prints, and textiles, making him one of the most versatile and prolific artists in history.

"The Weeping Woman" is a famous painting by Pablo Picasso, created in 1937. It depicts a woman crying and is part of Picasso's series of works on the theme of weeping women, inspired by the suffering caused by the Spanish Civil War. The painting is known for its emotional intensity and vivid colors.

"Girl before a Mirror" is another notable painting by Pablo Picasso, created in 1932. It portrays a woman looking at her reflection in a mirror, showing two different faces—one youthful and beautiful, the other distorted and aged. The painting explores themes of identity, perception, and self-reflection.
